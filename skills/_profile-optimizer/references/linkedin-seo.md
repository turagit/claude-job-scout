# LinkedIn SEO & Algorithm

## Recruiter Search Ranking Factors
1. **Keyword relevance** — exact/partial matches in headline, about, experience, skills
2. **Profile completeness** — All-Star profiles rank significantly higher
3. **Open to Work signal** — profiles with private recruiter signal enabled appear in filtered searches
4. **Network proximity** — 1st > 2nd > 3rd degree
5. **Activity recency** — recent activity (posts, comments, profile updates) boosts ranking
6. **Engagement signals** — profile views, response rate, SSI score
7. **Skills endorsements** — endorsed skills rank higher than unendorsed

## All-Star Requirements
Photo, custom headline, about (200+ words), current position with description, 2+ past positions, education, 5+ skills, 50+ connections, industry and location set.

## Social Selling Index (SSI)
- Check at `linkedin.com/sales/ssi`
- Four pillars: establishing brand (25), finding right people (25), engaging with insights (25), building relationships (25)
- Score 70+ significantly improves search ranking
- Updated daily based on LinkedIn activity

## Keyword Placement (by weight)
1. **Headline** — highest weight, always visible in search results and Google
2. **Current job title** — use searchable industry-standard terms
3. **About section** — natural keyword density, first 3 lines also appear in Google snippets
4. **Experience descriptions** — context for keywords, feeds ATS imports
5. **Skills section** — feeds recruiter skill filters directly; imported as structured tags by ATS systems (Greenhouse, Lever, Workday)

## Keyword Density
- Headline: 2-3 primary keywords
- About: primary 2-3x, secondary 1-2x
- Each experience entry: 3-5 keywords
- Skills: 30-50 as individual entries (use exact phrasing, not verbose forms)
- Total unique: 40-60 across profile

## ATS Import Behaviour
Many ATS systems allow recruiters to import LinkedIn profiles directly. When imported:
- **Skills** are pulled as structured tags — "Python" imports cleanly, "Python Programming Language" may not match the ATS taxonomy
- **Job titles** are used for experience matching — non-standard titles reduce match quality
- **Location** field maps to geographic filters — overly specific locations narrow the candidate pool
- **Industry** field maps to industry filters — must match target, not just current employer

## Google Discoverability
- LinkedIn profiles rank on page 1 of Google for most name searches
- Google displays: Name + Headline + first ~160 characters of About section
- Optimise headline and About opener to work as a Google search snippet
- Custom URL (`/in/firstname-lastname`) ranks better than default random slugs

## Recruiter Search Behaviour

### Recruiter Lite Filters
Recruiters using LinkedIn Recruiter Lite filter by: keywords, current title, current company, location, industry, school, profile language, Open to Work status.

### Sales Navigator / Recruiter Full Filters
Advanced filters add: years of experience, function, seniority level, company size, skills, groups, posted content keywords.

### How Recruiters Actually Search
1. Start with job title + 1-2 must-have skills as keywords
2. Filter by location (city, region, or country)
3. Filter by "Open to Work" candidates
4. Scan headlines and current titles in results (3-5 seconds per result)
5. Click profiles where headline signals relevance
6. Spend <10 seconds checking: current role, years of experience, key skills, location, availability
7. InMail or save candidates that pass the 10-second check

### Boolean Search Patterns
Recruiters commonly use Boolean on LinkedIn:
- `"data engineer" AND ("Apache Spark" OR "PySpark") AND "AWS"`
- `"frontend developer" AND "React" AND ("TypeScript" OR "JavaScript")`
- `"DevOps" AND ("Kubernetes" OR "Docker") AND "CI/CD" AND "remote"`
- Exact phrases in quotes are common — ensure your profile contains the exact phrases, not just individual words

## Activity & Algorithm Signals
- Posting 2-3x per week keeps profile active in algorithm
- Comments (substantive, 3+ sentences) on others' posts boost your visibility in their network
- Profile updates (even minor edits) signal activity
- Accepting connection requests quickly improves response rate metric
- Creator Mode surfaces posts to non-connections and adds topic hashtags to profile

## Common Mistakes
Creative job titles no one searches for, empty About section, <5 skills, default LinkedIn URL, no photo, inconsistent titles between CV and LinkedIn, Open to Work not enabled, location set too narrowly, industry field mismatched to target role, no activity for 30+ days.
