# Freelance / Contract Context

Shared adjustments applied across all skills when `user-profile.json` indicates freelance or contract work.

## Scoring Adjustments

| Dimension | Permanent Weight | Freelance Weight |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Skills Match | 30% | 35% |
| Experience Alignment | 25% | 20% |
| Requirements Fit | 25% | 30% |
| Growth & Culture | 10% | 5% |
| Practical Factors | 10% | 10% |

### Bonus/Penalty Factors

- **Contract duration:** 6-12 months +5, 1-3 months -5 (unless premium rate), unspecified flag "Duration not mentioned"
- **Rate transparency:** Rate stated +5 (sort above unlisted), salary stated +2, none flag "Does not mention rate"
- **IR35 (UK roles only):** Outside IR35 +5, inside IR35 flag (rate should compensate), not mentioned flag "IR35 status unclear"
- **Client vs agency:** Direct client +3, agency neutral but note agency name; look for end client in description
- **Start date:** "Immediate/ASAP" +3 if user available now

## Rate Normalization

Normalize all compensation to **daily rate** for comparison:
- Hourly x8 | Weekly /5 | Monthly /20 | Annual /220 (UK-standard: 220 working days/year)
- Note currency, never convert between currencies
- Freelance rates should be ~30-50% above permanent equivalent (no paid holidays, pension, benefits, gaps)

## Freelance CV Structure

- Project-based layout preferred over employer-based (client, role, duration, deliverables)
- Skills matrix at top for quick scanning
- Returning clients or extended contracts signal reliability
- Include: availability date, day rate range (optional), IR35 awareness, professional indemnity insurance if relevant

## Freelance Qualifying Questions (Priority Order)

1. Day rate or rate range?
2. Contract duration + extension potential?
3. Fully remote?
4. Inside or outside IR35? (UK only)
5. Start date urgency?
6. Direct client or via agency?
7. End client name? (if via agency)

## Freelance Response Tone

- Be direct about rate expectations — freelancers negotiate openly
- Ask about IR35 early (UK) — deal-breaker for many
- Mention availability window
- Keep responses professional but efficient — freelance hiring moves fast
