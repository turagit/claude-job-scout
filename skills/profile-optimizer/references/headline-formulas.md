# LinkedIn Headline Formulas

## Formula Patterns

### Pattern 1: Role + Specialty + Value
`[Role] | [Specialty Area] | [Value/Impact Statement]`
- "Senior Data Engineer | Real-Time Streaming & ML Pipelines | Turning Raw Data into Business Intelligence"
- "Product Manager | B2B SaaS | Driving 0→1 Product Launches"

### Pattern 2: Role + Skills + Industry
`[Role] specializing in [Skill 1] & [Skill 2] for [Industry]`
- "Full-Stack Developer specializing in React & Node.js for FinTech"
- "Marketing Director specializing in Growth & Brand for SaaS Startups"

### Pattern 3: Role + Keyword Stack
`[Role] | [Keyword] | [Keyword] | [Keyword] | [Keyword]`
- "DevOps Engineer | AWS | Kubernetes | Terraform | CI/CD"
- "UX Designer | User Research | Figma | Design Systems | Accessibility"

### Pattern 4: Impact-Led
`[Achievement/Impact] | [Role] at [Company/Type]`
- "Scaled Platform to 10M Users | Engineering Lead at Series B Startups"
- "Generated $5M+ in Pipeline | Growth Marketing Manager"

### Pattern 5: Dual Role / Career Transition
`[Target Role] | Background in [Previous Field] | [Bridging Skill]`
- "Aspiring Data Scientist | 8 Years in Financial Analysis | Python & ML"

## Headline Do's
- Use all 220 characters
- Include at least 2 searchable keywords
- Make the first 60 characters count (that's what shows in most previews)
- Update headline when changing job search targets

## Headline Don'ts
- "Open to Work" as the entire headline (use the LinkedIn badge instead)
- Buzzwords without substance: "Passionate Thought Leader"
- Current company only: "Employee at Company X"
- Self-promotional fluff: "Award-Winning Visionary Disruptor"
