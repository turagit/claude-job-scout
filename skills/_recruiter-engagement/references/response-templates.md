# Recruiter Response Templates

## Initial Responses

**Hot Lead — Express Interest + Qualify:**
Thank them, reference specific role/company alignment with your experience, ask top 2-3 qualifying questions (compensation, remote, team), suggest a call.

**Warm Lead — Positive + Info Request:**
Thank them, express openness to learn more, ask for JD and details on compensation/work arrangement before going further.

**Cold Lead — Polite Decline:**
Thank them, acknowledge the outreach, explain it's not the right fit, offer to stay connected for future relevant opportunities.

## Follow-Ups

**No Response (5-7 days):** Brief follow-up referencing original message, restate interest, offer scheduling flexibility.

**After Receiving JD:** Confirm you reviewed it, highlight strongest alignment area, ask 1-2 specific follow-up questions, express interest in next steps.

**After Phone Screen:** Thank them, reference specific topic discussed, express enthusiasm, confirm readiness for next steps.

## Handling Scenarios

**Salary Question:** Express flexibility, ask for budgeted range first to ensure alignment.

**Immediate Availability:** State current status and earliest start date, ask about their timeline.

**Agency — Need Details:** Ask for company name (avoid conflicts), full JD, and compensation range before discussing further.

**Multiple Recruiters Same Company:** Be transparent about existing conversations, ask if this is a different position.

## Freelance Templates

**Hot Lead:** Express interest, ask day rate range, duration + extension potential, IR35 status, remote status. Mention availability date.

**Warm Lead:** Ask for rate range, duration, IR35, remote/on-site, and end client (if agency) before committing time.

**Rate Negotiation:** Acknowledge interest, state the offered rate is below range, cite experience and market rate, ask about budget flexibility.

**Availability Check:** State current contract end date, confirm earliest availability, ask if timeline works.
